Monthly Lessons- Dec 2014

Following are the topics that will be covered during class sessions this month inshAllah. We will not have any classes on Friday, Dec 26th. For previous months' lessons please visit our website at www.schaumburgareamuslimevents.org

Mawlana Asif's Class(Boys 13+): ...
1) Duas (Best in this life and hereafter, sleeping, and experiencing unrest or fear during sleep),
2) Facts about Rabiul Awwal
3) Circle Gathering (Topic will be decided by Students)
4) Ta'lim al-Deen by M. Ashraf Ali Thanwi (beliefs 30-35)

Alhamdulilah, last month we were able to talk about racism and how can we fix the problem of racism within our selves. Students gave situations in which racism takes place, then they came up with solutions. I encourage all of the parents to ask their children after every class what they learned in class because it will help them remember and implement what they learned into there lives.

Br. Farhan's Class (Boys 9-12)

Have the students learn the correct method of doing wudhu and then preform it with the teacher watching.

Discuss with students how to make our winter break productive.

Discuss the diseases of the heart such as lust, jealousy, and greed.

Continue to watch the Inspiration series with Shaykh Omer Suleiman and discuss practical lessons we can take away from it.

Discuss some of the issues our student's are facing in school.

Discuss what it means to be a Muslim.


Sr. Sabaahath's class(Girls 12+)

Dec 5- Open discussion- these topics were dicussed: Peer relationships/bullying/internet safety

Dec 12- Art project, (continuation of calligraphy project), Importance of forgiveness. Personal safety and recognizing what is inappropriate in terms of social interactions.

Dec 19- Self-respect and respect for others

Dec 26- No Class

Restoration Workday With Dupage County Forest Preserve

Al-Huda Youth Program has participated in Restoration Workday with the Dupage County Forest Preserve on Nov 9th. Our Youth worked very hard in removing non-native, invasive vegetation. They also planted many seeds of native plants and trees to promote new growth. It was a wonderful opportunity to show the Muslim presence in our area. After all the hard work, our students enjoyed delicious hot pizza at Italian Express!
Br. Sohaib Quadri had prepped up the students prior to the event with a motivational talk on implementing the sunnah of preservation. He also emphasized on representing the Muslim community in a non-muslim environment through positive efforts.
